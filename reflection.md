# Pairwise Profile Reflections

I compared outputs for all profile pairs to check whether recommendation changes match user intent.

1. High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi: High-Energy Pop pushes upbeat tracks like Sunrise City, while Chill Lofi shifts to calm, acoustic-leaning tracks like Library Rain. This makes sense because energy and acoustic targets move in opposite directions.
2. High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock: Both like high energy, but Pop gets brighter dance songs while Rock gets heavier intense songs. Same energy, different genre and mood priorities.
3. High-Energy Pop vs Conflicting Vibe: High-Energy Pop gets cheerful club-like songs, while Conflicting Vibe surfaces sadder acoustic tracks like Blue Lantern. Mood preference (happy vs melancholic) clearly changes the list.
4. High-Energy Pop vs Ultra Chill: High-Energy Pop favors energetic songs; Ultra Chill drops into peaceful low-energy songs like Golden Hour Strings. This is exactly what target energy should do.
5. High-Energy Pop vs Pure Adrenaline: Both want high energy, but Pure Adrenaline pushes even harder into extreme-energy EDM/metal like Laser Marathon. The higher energy target and aggressive mood explain the shift.
6. High-Energy Pop vs Acoustic Rocker: High-Energy Pop rewards low acoustic pop songs, while Acoustic Rocker still keeps rock tracks high despite unusual acoustic preference. Genre weight keeps rock near the top for the rocker profile.
7. High-Energy Pop vs Everything Goes: High-Energy Pop is more focused and predictable, while Everything Goes mixes pop, rock, and lofi in one list. Broader genre/mood lists naturally widen outcomes.

8. Chill Lofi vs Deep Intense Rock: Chill Lofi pulls soft and focused songs; Deep Intense Rock pulls hard-driving intense songs. Opposite energy and mood goals create very different outputs.
9. Chill Lofi vs Conflicting Vibe: Both can include acoustic songs, but Chill Lofi stays calm/focused while Conflicting Vibe mixes sadness with high energy tension. Mood target explains the difference.
10. Chill Lofi vs Ultra Chill: Both are calm and acoustic-friendly, but Ultra Chill pushes even lower energy songs first. Lower energy target makes the list more serene.
11. Chill Lofi vs Pure Adrenaline: Chill Lofi and Pure Adrenaline are near opposites: one wants relaxed low-mid energy, the other wants max intensity. Results split cleanly between lofi/ambient and EDM/metal.
12. Chill Lofi vs Acoustic Rocker: Chill Lofi keeps lofi/ambient at the top, while Acoustic Rocker keeps rock/metal higher even with acoustic preference. Genre identity still matters strongly.
13. Chill Lofi vs Everything Goes: Chill Lofi is narrow and calm; Everything Goes is mixed and often includes upbeat pop/indie-pop. Broad preferences reduce specialization.

14. Deep Intense Rock vs Conflicting Vibe: Deep Intense Rock rewards aggression and intensity; Conflicting Vibe prefers melancholic/blues tones. Both can include strong songs, but emotional direction changes the picks.
15. Deep Intense Rock vs Ultra Chill: Deep Intense Rock selects high-energy heavy songs; Ultra Chill selects very low-energy peaceful tracks. This confirms energy targeting is doing meaningful work.
16. Deep Intense Rock vs Pure Adrenaline: Both like intensity, but Pure Adrenaline leans more EDM/euphoric while Deep Intense Rock keeps rock/metal/hip-hop flavor. Mood and genre ordering split them.
17. Deep Intense Rock vs Acoustic Rocker: Both share rock roots, but Acoustic Rocker allows more acoustic-leaning balance and less pure aggression. Same genre family, different texture preference.
18. Deep Intense Rock vs Everything Goes: Deep Intense Rock stays concentrated in heavy genres, while Everything Goes includes lighter options too. Broad profiles should look less extreme.

19. Conflicting Vibe vs Ultra Chill: Conflicting Vibe combines sadness with high energy tension, while Ultra Chill aims for peaceful low-energy calm. Different moods and energy targets create distinct lists.
20. Conflicting Vibe vs Pure Adrenaline: Both can contain high energy songs, but Conflicting Vibe is darker/melancholic and more acoustic, while Pure Adrenaline is euphoric and non-acoustic. Mood and acoustic targets explain it.
21. Conflicting Vibe vs Acoustic Rocker: Conflicting Vibe often pulls blues/jazz mood tracks; Acoustic Rocker keeps rock/metal identity. Genre anchors differ even when acoustic preference can overlap.
22. Conflicting Vibe vs Everything Goes: Conflicting Vibe is emotionally specific, Everything Goes is broad and balanced. Specific emotional intent leads to a narrower, mood-heavy list.

23. Ultra Chill vs Pure Adrenaline: This is the clearest opposite pair: ultra-low calm versus near-max adrenaline. Recommendation lists separate almost completely.
24. Ultra Chill vs Acoustic Rocker: Ultra Chill emphasizes peaceful and low energy; Acoustic Rocker still keeps more energetic rock tracks visible. Genre preference prevents full convergence.
25. Ultra Chill vs Everything Goes: Ultra Chill narrows to calm/acoustic corners, while Everything Goes mixes multiple energies and genres. Broad profiles naturally include more variety.

26. Pure Adrenaline vs Acoustic Rocker: Pure Adrenaline pushes fast, loud, non-acoustic songs; Acoustic Rocker keeps rock identity but can tolerate more acoustic texture. Different acoustic and mood priorities drive the gap.
27. Pure Adrenaline vs Everything Goes: Pure Adrenaline is extreme and focused; Everything Goes includes moderate songs as well. Energy target and flexibility produce the difference.

28. Acoustic Rocker vs Everything Goes: Acoustic Rocker still centers on rock mood/intensity, while Everything Goes blends across genres and moods. One is identity-driven; the other is exploration-driven.

Plain-language note on "Gym Hero": it keeps showing up for Happy Pop because it sits in a sweet spot the model rewards: high energy, low acousticness, and decent danceability. Even if its mood tag is not always the top mood, those strong feature matches keep its total score high.
